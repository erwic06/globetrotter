Top Attractions

Three stops that show off Hawaii's range — reef, rainforest, and ridgeline.

Hanauma Bay

A protected marine reserve curved into an old volcanic crater. The shallow, calm water makes it the most beginner-friendly snorkeling on Oahu — expect parrotfish, sea turtles, and a short conservation video before you can enter.

Waimea Valley

A gentle, mostly paved walk through botanical gardens that ends at a 60-foot waterfall you can swim under. It's a quieter, greener counterpoint to the beaches, and a good rainy-day backup on the North Shore.

Diamond Head

The crater hike everyone photographs. It's a steep but short climb with stairs and a tunnel near the top, rewarding you with a panorama over Waikiki and the south shore. Go at sunrise to beat the heat and the crowds.
