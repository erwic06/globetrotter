Seafood Guide

If you only learn one thing here: eat the fish. Three spots, three kinds of traveler.

Giovanni's Shrimp Truck

Best for: backpackers and road-trippers

66-472 Kamehameha Hwy, Haleiwa, HI

A graffiti-covered truck slinging garlic shrimp plates on the North Shore. Cash-friendly, huge portions, picnic-table seating — the no-fuss, high-reward stop on a circle-island drive.

Ono Seafood

Best for: solo travelers and quick lunches

747 Kapahulu Ave, Honolulu, HI

A tiny counter that many locals call the best poke in Honolulu. Order the shoyu ahi, grab a seat outside, and don't be surprised by a line — it moves fast and it's worth it.

Nico's Pier 38

Best for: families and sit-down dinners

1129 N Nimitz Hwy, Honolulu, HI

A harbor-side restaurant and fish market right where the boats come in. Roomy seating, a kids-friendly menu, and fish that was swimming that morning. Come for the furikake-crusted ahi.
